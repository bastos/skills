# App Store Review Cross-Checks

Official source: https://developer.apple.com/app-store/review/guidelines/

Use this file only as a compliance cross-check. Do not cite it as Human Interface Guidelines. Apply it when UI, copy, onboarding, permissions, payment flows, metadata, or screenshots create App Store review risk.

## Cross-Check Areas

- **Safety**: Flag prank, false, misleading, medical, children, support/contact, or user-generated-content flows that could harm people or misrepresent the app.
- **Performance and metadata**: App screenshots, previews, descriptions, feature names, and paywall copy should accurately reflect the core experience and avoid hidden, dormant, undocumented, or misleading functionality.
- **Business and payments**: Digital goods, subscriptions, entitlements, Apple Pay, recurring payments, and purchase disclosure need the appropriate Apple flow and clear material terms before purchase.
- **Design and minimum functionality**: The app should offer enough native value and functionality to stand on its own; flag placeholder shells, thin wrappers, incomplete settings-only containers, or flows that feel unfinished.
- **Privacy and legal**: Privacy policy access, permission rationale, location use, child data, HealthKit/medical data, trademarks, Apple product resemblance, Apple marks, attribution, and third-party rights matter when visible in UI.

## How to Report

- Label these findings as `App Store Review cross-check`, not HIG.
- Tie the finding to the visible UI/copy and explain the submission or trust risk.
- If exact policy wording matters, open the current App Store Review Guidelines page live before making the claim.
