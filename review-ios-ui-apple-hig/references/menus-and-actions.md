## Review Checks

- Match the action surface to intent: direct buttons for primary commands, menus for secondary or grouped commands, context menus for object-specific actions, edit menus for text/editing actions, and activity views for sharing or system extensions.
- Keep primary actions visible and unambiguous. Do not bury the main task in a menu.
- Keep destructive actions separated, clearly labeled, and recoverable through confirmation or undo when consequences are hard to reverse.
- Use concise verb-first labels where possible. Avoid labels that only make sense with surrounding visual context.
- Keep action availability honest: disabled, loading, permission-required, and unavailable states should be visible and explainable when needed.
- Preserve expected gestures and affordances: row actions, back gestures, long-press/context behavior, toolbar placement, and share sheet behavior should match platform expectations.
- For Home Screen quick actions, expose durable, high-value shortcuts rather than transient or rarely used commands.
